# Executive Brief: Nordic Food System Concentration

**For:** NCH Board / Nordic Innovation evaluators
**From:** Food Systems Transition Group (Gabriel Freeman & Cathrine Barth)
**Date:** March 2026 | **Status:** Based on Draft v1.1

---

## The Problem

Three companies control 93.4% of Norway's grocery stores (HHI 3,438 — 37.5% above the "highly concentrated" threshold). All four Nordic markets exceed international concentration benchmarks. This is not an aberration — it is a **stable attractor state** reinforced by closed logistics networks, purchasing power asymmetries, and vertical integration.

## Key Findings

| Finding | Evidence |
|---------|----------|
| **Norwegian triopoly is structural** | HHI 3,438; top-3 share 93.4% (stores) / ~97% (revenue); stable 25+ years |
| **Hidden value extraction** | Producer prices +48.7% since 2020 vs. consumer +33.2% — extraction via opaque fees, not shelf prices |
| **Seasonal margin expansion** | July consumer-producer price gap quintupled: 0.83 pts (2015) → 4.23 pts (2025) |
| **Norway is the Nordic extreme** | Highest top-3 share, lowest self-sufficiency (44%), no international competitors |
| **Denmark proves alternatives exist** | 5 players, declining concentration, net food exporter (~300% self-sufficiency) |
| **Finland has the strongest response** | Automatic dominance at 30% market share (§4a, since 2014) |
| **Fines don't change structure** | NOK 4.9B Prisjeger fine (Aug 2024) — historic, but system unchanged |

## Norway's Double Vulnerability

- **Import-dependent:** 44% caloric self-sufficiency; 96% of fruit imported
- **Distribution-dependent:** Three closed logistics networks serve 93% of stores
- A simultaneous supply disruption and logistics failure would cascade rapidly

## Five Transition Levers

1. **Finnish-style automatic dominance provision** — NorgesGruppen (44%) would immediately qualify
2. **Open logistics access** — regulated third-party access to distribution infrastructure
3. **Strengthened UTP enforcement** — resist Dagligvaretilsynet abolition; protect complainants
4. **Structural barrier removal** — address planning/zoning barriers to new store entry
5. **Food security integration** — link concentration policy to emergency preparedness
