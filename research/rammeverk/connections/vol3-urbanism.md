## 2. The Modernist Error: Le Corbusier & Robert Moses
*The "McNamara's Fallacy" of Urbanism.*

### Le Corbusier's "Radiant City"
*   **The Vision:** Demolish the messy, historical city center. Replace it with identical high-rise towers set in a park.
*   **The Error:** He removed the *street*—the chaotic, messy place where human connection happens. He treated the city as a visual object to be viewed from an airplane, not a place to be lived in.
*   **Legacy:** The housing projects of the 20th century (the "Projects") are direct descendants of this failed theory.

### Robert Moses (The Power Broker)
*   **The Action:** He bulldozed the Bronx to build the Cross Bronx Expressway.
*   **The Metric:** He optimized for "Vehicle Miles Traveled" (Speed). He destroyed neighborhoods (Territory) to improve the map of the highway system.
*   **Connection:** Just as McNamara used "Body Counts," Moses used "Traffic Flow." Both ignored the qualitative reality of the system they were managing.

## 3. The Regenerative Response: Christopher Alexander
*The "Project Cybersyn" of Urbanism.*

### A Pattern Language (1977)
*   **The Concept:** You cannot "design" a city. You can only provide the "DNA" (Patterns) and let the city grow itself.
*   **The Patterns:** 253 rules for human well-being (e.g., "Light on Two Sides of Every Room," "Street Cafe," "Accessible Green").
*   **The Process:** **Piecemal Growth.** A city should not be built in master-planned "Phases"; it should grow incrementally, repairing itself as it goes.
*   **Quote:** *"A building or a town is given its character, essentially, by those events which keep on happening there most often."*

## 4. The Oslo Context: A Fractal City?
*Applying the theory to our subject.*

*   **Historic Oslo:** Grew organically around the harbor (Power Law distribution of streets).
*   **Modern Oslo:** The "Barcode" project and Fjord City. Is it a "Radiant City" (Glass Towers) or a "Pattern Language" (Public Access)?
*   **The Dashboard Mission:** To measure the "Vitality" (Jane Jacobs) of Oslo, not just its "Efficiency." To show where the "Organic City" is thriving and where the "Machine City" is suffocating it.
