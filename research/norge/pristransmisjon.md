# Data Insight: Price Transmission Analysis (2020-2025)

**Date:** December 30, 2025
**Source:** SSB Tables 03013 (KPI) and 12462 (PPI)
**Methodology:** Rebased both indices to Jan 2020 = 100 to observe cumulative inflation since the start of the pandemic/inflationary cycle.

## Key Findings

### 1. The "Reverse" Gap
Contrary to the popular "Greedflation" hypothesis (that retailers hiked prices faster than costs), the data shows that **Producer Prices (PPI)** have risen significantly faster than **Consumer Prices (KPI)** since 2020.

*   **PPI Growth (Jan 2020 - Nov 2025):** +42.7%
*   **KPI Growth (Jan 2020 - Nov 2025):** +32.5%
*   **Spread:** -10.2 points

### 2. Interpretation
This data suggests two possibilities:
1.  **Margin Compression:** Retailers have absorbed a portion of the raw material cost shock, preventing the full magnitude of the "Cost Tsunami" from hitting consumers.
2.  **Lag Effect:** Consumer prices are trailing producer prices and may continue to rise even if PPI stabilizes.
3.  **Composition Mismatch:** The "Food Industry" PPI includes volatile elements (like energy-intensive processing) that affect the index more than the final consumer basket.

### 3. Impact on Nordstad Thesis
This finding **complicates** Nordstad's narrative. If retailers were aggressively expanding margins via simple price gouging, KPI should track or exceed PPI. The fact that it lags suggests the "Power" (Matmakt) might be exercised differently:
*   **Squeezing Suppliers (Back-end):** The "price" captured in PPI might be the *invoice price*, but retailers might be demanding higher "Joint Marketing" fees or "Bonuses" which are *deducted* later, effectively lowering the real cost below the PPI figure.
*   **Volume over Margin:** Retailers might be accepting lower % margins to maintain market share.

## Conclusion
The "Smoking Gun" is not in the simple shelf price vs. factory gate price. The visible margins appear to have compressed. The "Super-Profits" identified by the Competition Authority must therefore reside in:
1.  **Fixed Fees / Kickbacks** (not captured in unit prices).
2.  **Wholesale/Logistics Profits** (transfer pricing within the group).
3.  **Volume Efficiency** (selling 42% more expensive goods at slightly lower margin still yields higher *absolute* profit).

**Status:** Quantitative evidence suggests the mechanism of profit extraction is opaque (hidden fees), not transparent (price gouging).
