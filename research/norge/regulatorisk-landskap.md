# Research Addendum II: Regulatory & Structural Landscape (2024-2025)
**Supplement to: The Cost of Control (Nordstad Thesis)**
**Date:** December 30, 2025
**Focus:** Dagligvaretilsynet, Lov om god handelsskikk, and Vertical Integration Updates

---

## 1. Regulatory Efficacy: Dagligvaretilsynet & "The Law"

### 1.1 Status of "Lov om god handelsskikk" (2023-2024)
*   **Awareness vs. Action:** While awareness of the law has reached near-saturation (89% of suppliers, 95% of chains), the *Dagligvaretilsynet* reports a persistent "fear culture."
*   **The "Silence" Problem:** Despite high awareness, the Inspectorate receives very few concrete formal complaints. This validates Nordstad’s "Matmakt" theory: suppliers are too afraid of delisting (retaliation) to use the legal tools available to them.
*   **2025 Evaluation:** A comprehensive evaluation of the law was planned but postponed to 2025 due to resource constraints, delaying potential strengthening of the regulations.

### 1.2 The "Private Label" (EMV) Loophole
*   **Regulatory Gap:** The Inspectorate explicitly noted in 2023 that § 9 of the law needs a clearer definition of EMV to prevent "copycat" products that undermine independent brands. This aligns with Nordstad’s claim that EMV is used to cannibalize innovation.

## 2. Vertical Integration: The "Quiet" Expansion (2023-2024)

### 2.1 No "Big Bang," Just "Creep"
Unlike the historic acquisitions of the past (e.g., ICA), the 2023-2024 period has not seen massive vertical acquisitions. Instead, integration has deepened through *subsidiary expansion*:
*   **Coop:** Established *Himmelgrønt AS* (2023) to control salad/vegetable production, further reducing reliance on independent Bama/Gartnerhallen structures.
*   **Rema 1000:** Continued expansion of *Kolly* (established 2021) to vertically integrate the "Storhusholdning" (Catering/Horeca) market, locking in distribution not just to stores but to restaurants/canteens.
*   **NorgesGruppen:** Deepened control via *Bama* (46% ownership) and logistics dominance.

### 2.2 Strategic Implication
The lack of headline-grabbing buyouts suggests the chains have already achieved "Structural Peak." They don't need to buy more food factories; they simply need to optimize the volume flowing through the ones they already control (or control via exclusive contracts).

---

## 3. Synthesis with Nordstad Thesis
*   **Regulatory Failure:** The "Lov om god handelsskikk" acts as a shield that is too heavy to lift. The fear of retribution renders it functionally weak, supporting Nordstad’s view that "legal rights" mean little against "market might."
*   **The "Himmelgrønt" Case:** Coop’s move to start its own vegetable production is a classic example of Nordstad’s warning: chains insourcing production to capture the margin previously held by farmers/co-ops.

**Status:** The system is "locked in." Regulatory bodies are active but cautious; chains are deepening control through internal organic growth rather than external M&A.
