# Nordic Master's Theses: Grocery Market Concentration & Food Systems

Compiled 2026-03-13. Theses and student projects from Nordic universities relevant to understanding grocery market dynamics, competition, private labels, and food system transitions.

---

## DENMARK

### 1. Danish Grocery Retail: Does Preference Structure Influence the Perception of Price and Quality?
- **Authors:** Christie Nielsen & Ninna Aarfelt Andersen
- **Year:** 2016
- **Institution:** Copenhagen Business School (CBS)
- **Programme:** MSc in Economics and Marketing
- **Language:** Danish
- **URL:** https://research.cbs.dk/en/studentProjects/danish-grocery-retail-does-preference-structure-influence-the-per/
- **Description:** Survey of 506 Danish consumers identifying four buyer segments (Average Joes, Premiuers, Value seekers, Discounters). Finds discount supermarkets expanding beyond low-price positioning while conventional supermarkets are "stuck in the middle." Shows how preference structure influences perception of price and quality in Danish grocery stores.

### 2. Optimering af konkurrenceevnen: Et grounded theory af det danske dagligvaremarked
- **Authors:** Brian Nowak Jensen
- **Year:** 2009
- **Institution:** Copenhagen Business School (CBS)
- **Programme:** MSc in Supply Chain Management
- **Language:** Danish
- **URL:** https://research.cbs.dk/en/studentProjects/bdc76340-a660-43ac-86c8-61ac6de897f9
- **Description:** Grounded theory study of COOP Danmark through observations and interviews with shop managers. Finds the Danish grocery market is "very conservative" and lacks competitive concern regarding foreign entrants. Recommends technology investment and integrated supply chain coordination.

### 3. Assessment of Grocery eCommerce in Scandinavia
- **Authors:** Julija Minkeviciute
- **Year:** 2019
- **Institution:** Copenhagen Business School (CBS)
- **Programme:** MSc in Business Administration and E-business
- **Language:** English
- **URL:** https://research.cbs.dk/en/studentProjects/assessment-of-grocery-ecommerce-in-scandinavia
- **Description:** Compares grocery e-commerce potential across Denmark, Sweden, and Norway. Identifies barriers and business models through semi-structured interviews with retail and FMCG experts. Cross-Nordic comparative study.

### 4. The internationalization of a premium grocery retail brand: A case study of Irma and the Swedish grocery retail market
- **Authors:** Sarah Loftager Pottenger & Marianne Sorensen
- **Year:** 2009
- **Institution:** Copenhagen Business School (CBS)
- **Programme:** Cand.merc.kom (Business Economics and Corporate Communication)
- **Language:** English
- **URL:** https://research.cbs.dk/da/studentProjects/246c0f0a-12de-4730-8d93-f3ed35dae3b8
- **Description:** Examines how Danish premium grocery retailer Irma could expand into Sweden. Identifies an unoccupied market position in Sweden for Irma's brand DNA of quality, service, ecology, and social responsibility. Recommends "brand glocalization" strategy.

### 5. Analyse af Salling Group A/S og Coop Danmark A/S
- **Authors:** Andy Huynh & Ida Elisabeth Mortensen
- **Year:** 2025
- **Institution:** Aalborg University (AAU)
- **Programme:** HD 1. del (Erhvervsokonomisk metode)
- **Language:** Danish
- **URL:** https://projekter.aau.dk/projekter/files/783577089/Afgangsprojekt_HD1_Analyse_af_Salling_Group_AS_og_Coop_Danmark_AS.pdf
- **Description:** Comparative financial and strategic analysis of Denmark's two largest grocery retailers using CVR data and annual reports 2018-2023, plus consumer interviews at Coop365 and Fotex. Very recent and directly relevant to current Danish market dynamics.

## SWEDEN

### 6. The Swedish food retail market: An econometric analysis of competition on local food retail markets
- **Authors:** Malin Blank & Anna Maria Persson
- **Year:** 2004
- **Institution:** Linkoping University, Department of Management and Economics
- **Programme:** Master's in Economics
- **Language:** Swedish
- **URL:** http://www.diva-portal.org/smash/record.jsf?pid=diva2%3A19856
- **Description:** Econometric model analyzing ICA's performance across local markets. Finds concentration is "high and steadily increasing" with three companies controlling ~75% of market share. Concludes retailers rely minimally on price/service/quality differentiation.

### 7. Competition in the Swedish Food Retail Industry: An empirical pilot study estimating the mean price-cost markup
- **Author:** Robin Myte
- **Year:** 2012
- **Institution:** Umea University, School of Business and Economics
- **Programme:** Bachelor of Science (Economics)
- **Language:** English
- **URL:** http://www.diva-portal.org/smash/record.jsf?pid=diva2%3A537636
- **Description:** Empirical estimation of price-cost markups in Swedish food retail. Directly measures the degree of competition/market power in the oligopolistic Swedish grocery market.

### 8. Svensk dagligvaruhandel: forandringar och strategier for markesvaruleverantorer
- **Authors:** Eric Ponzio, Joakim Wulff & Martin Larsson
- **Year:** 2004
- **Institution:** Lund University, School of Economics and Management
- **Programme:** Master's Degree (One Year)
- **Language:** Swedish
- **URL:** https://lup.lub.lu.se/student-papers/search/publication/1350287
- **Description:** Examines how Swedish grocery retail chains strengthened market positions through store networks, centralized decisions, and private labels. Finds chains became "direct competitors" to suppliers through private label investments. Analyzes impact on branded goods suppliers' strategies.

### 9. Globalisering inom dagligvaruhandeln: Har svenska aktorer en chans?
- **Authors:** Karin Demuth, Carolin Bengtsson & Anna Bayer
- **Year:** 2002
- **Institution:** Lund University, School of Economics and Management
- **Programme:** Master's Degree (One Year)
- **Language:** Swedish
- **URL:** https://lup.lub.lu.se/student-papers/search/publication/1340985
- **Description:** Analyzes globalization's impact on Swedish grocery retail. Examines consolidation, store concept standardization, and shifting power dynamics in supply chains. Concludes market adaptation and implementation capability provide more sustainable competitive advantages than logistics/branding resources.

### 10. Premium Private Label Market Growth on the Expense of National Brands
- **Author:** Jakob Ohlson
- **Year:** 2019
- **Institution:** Jonkoping University (JIBS), Economics Department
- **Programme:** Master's (One Year), 30 HE credits
- **Language:** English
- **URL:** https://www.diva-portal.org/smash/record.jsf?pid=diva2%3A1320178
- **Description:** Regression analysis of 2018 cross-sectional retail data examining factors driving premium private label market growth in Sweden's FMCG sector. Finds significant positive effects in food categories (coffee, poultry) and from organic product shares. Product variety shows negative correlation with premium PL adoption.

### 11. Private label in the supermarket industry: a case study of ICA
- **Authors:** Zhibing Mo & Yulan Qu
- **Year:** 2011
- **Institution:** University of Gavle, Department of Business and Economic Studies
- **Programme:** Business Administration (Bachelor level)
- **Language:** English
- **URL:** https://www.diva-portal.org/smash/record.jsf?pid=diva2%3A441195
- **Description:** Case study examining why ICA introduced private labels. Finds ICA released private labels "to counter manufacturer power and to be more price competitive." Private label strategy positively influenced customer loyalty and brand recognition.

### 12. The Swedish Organic Food Market: A Competitor and Industry Analysis
- **Authors:** Samuel Gauger, Jonas Deichmann & Sarah Fitz-Koch
- **Year:** 2012
- **Institution:** Jonkoping University (JIBS)
- **Programme:** Business Administration (Bachelor level)
- **Language:** English
- **URL:** https://www.diva-portal.org/smash/record.jsf?pid=diva2%3A530452
- **Description:** Competitive analysis of Sweden's organic food sector. Finds "ICA and Coop are the main competitors followed by Axfood" with minimal differentiation. ICA pursues cost leadership; Coop emphasizes differentiation; independent retailers maintain product differentiation.

### 13. Samarbetet mellan leverantor och dagligvaruhandel vid tillverkning av EMV (Private labels in Swedish dairy)
- **Author:** Annika Svennung
- **Year:** 2008
- **Institution:** SLU, Department of Economics
- **Programme:** Agriculture Programme (270 HEC)
- **Language:** Swedish
- **URL:** https://stud.epsilon.slu.se/10915/
- **Description:** Examines coordination between manufacturers and retailers in Swedish private label dairy production. Finds Swedish dairies primarily produce "me-too" private labels. Shows closer relationships between retailers and Swedish dairies compared to foreign suppliers.

### 14. EMV-utvecklingens effekter pa mjolkproducenterna
- **Authors:** Anna Berge & Charlotte Larsson
- **Year:** 2013
- **Institution:** SLU, Department of Economics
- **Programme:** Agricultural Programme - Economics and Management
- **Language:** Swedish
- **URL:** https://stud.epsilon.slu.se/5877/
- **Description:** Examines how private label development affects Swedish dairy farmers. Uses transaction cost and agency theory. Finds when dairies receive lower payments for private label goods, company results decline, reducing what milk suppliers earn. Long-term risks emerge when retailers leverage market power.

### 15. How do private labels affect innovation in the food industry?
- **Author:** Sofia Glimvall
- **Year:** 2015
- **Institution:** SLU, Department of Food Science
- **Programme:** Food - Innovation and Market Master's Programme
- **Language:** English
- **URL:** https://stud.epsilon.slu.se/8204/
- **Description:** Examines how private label competition impacts food sector innovation. Finds increases in private label shares "could impede innovation within the food industry" by reducing manufacturer investment. Smaller manufacturers face particular vulnerability.

### 16. The Future of Organic Food in Swedish Retail
- **Author:** Anne Dominique Vos
- **Year:** 2022
- **Institution:** SLU, Dept. of People and Society
- **Programme:** Sustainable Food Systems - Master's Programme
- **Language:** English
- **URL:** https://stud.epsilon.slu.se/17972/
- **Description:** Examines why Sweden's organic food market share growth halted after 2016. Organic now competes within broader "Sustainability food category." Recommends new labeling systems and an overarching sustainability initiative to align conflicting government agency objectives.

### 17. Hybrid Supply Chain Models in Swedish Grocery Retail
- **Authors:** Emilia Sedwall, Alexander Grandin & Calle Bjerne
- **Year:** 2025
- **Institution:** Jonkoping University (JIBS)
- **Programme:** Business Administration
- **Language:** English
- **URL:** http://www.diva-portal.org/smash/record.jsf?pid=diva2%3A1966137
- **Description:** Examines how hybrid distribution in Swedish grocery retail reduces emissions and drives cost savings. Seven semi-structured interviews with employees at various levels. Recent thesis applying Triple Bottom Line Framework to Swedish grocery logistics.

### 18. Relationship Marketing in the FMCG: The Forgotten Consumer
- **Author:** Philip Gilchrist
- **Year:** 2015
- **Institution:** Linkoping University, Department of Management and Engineering
- **Programme:** Master Thesis in Business and Economics
- **Language:** English
- **URL:** https://www.diva-portal.org/smash/record.jsf?pid=diva2%3A824204
- **Description:** Five case studies with Swedish FMCG producers. Finds producers "merely make any use of Relationship Marketing towards consumers" despite significant resources for B2B relationship marketing. Highlights pressure from powerful retailers and discount chains.

### 19. AI in the Swedish Food System
- **Authors:** Amirhadi Zakeri & Yiming Lei
- **Year:** 2024
- **Institution:** Uppsala University, Department of Civil and Industrial Engineering
- **Programme:** Master Programme in Industrial Management and Innovation
- **Language:** English
- **URL:** https://uu.diva-portal.org/smash/record.jsf?pid=diva2%3A1872486
- **Description:** Investigates AI implementation in Swedish food production using Socio-Technical System Theory. Identifies AI adoption is in "early adopter phase." Highlights barriers including unsuitable business models and fragmented data infrastructures. Relevant for food system modernization context.

### 20. Matkooperativ: affarsmodeller och drivkrafter (Food cooperatives)
- **Author:** Karoline Johansson
- **Year:** 2018
- **Institution:** Lund University
- **Programme:** Bachelor Degree, Environmental Science
- **Language:** Swedish
- **URL:** https://lup.lub.lu.se/student-papers/search/publication/8945415
- **Description:** Examines how food cooperatives function as alternatives to concentrated retail. Analyzes three operational models. Finds willingness to support sustainable food production and social aspects are key motivators. Relevant as counter-model to oligopolistic grocery retail.

## FINLAND

### 21. Consumer-owned retail cooperative in duopoly with horizontally differentiated goods: A Finnish experience
- **Author:** Tuomas Kronqvist
- **Year:** 2010
- **Institution:** Aalto University, Department of Economics
- **Programme:** Master's thesis (Economics)
- **Language:** English
- **URL:** https://aaltodoc.aalto.fi/handle/123456789/523
- **Description:** KEY THESIS. Analyzes Finland's concentrated grocery market (S-group 43%, K-group 34%) as a duopoly where the market leader is a consumer cooperative. Constructs a Bertrand competition model finding the cooperative prices at marginal cost, reducing adverse effects of duopoly. Consumers benefit from mixed duopoly structure. Directly relevant to understanding Finland's unique market structure.

### 22. Exploring the significance of digital disruption on the Finnish grocery industry
- **Author:** Maria Kupila
- **Year:** 2020
- **Institution:** Aalto University, Department of Management
- **Programme:** International Design Business Management
- **Language:** English
- **URL:** https://aaltodoc.aalto.fi/handle/123456789/44246
- **Description:** Qualitative study (20 interviews) with suppliers, retailers, and consumers on digitalization in Finland's concentrated grocery sector. Explores online channels' potential role in disrupting the concentrated market. Identifies supply and demand-side obstacles to e-grocery.

### 23. Promoting competition in Finnish grocery trade: case Kaufland Stiftung & Co. KG
- **Author:** Finn Wendland
- **Year:** 2012
- **Institution:** Lahti University of Applied Sciences
- **Programme:** Business Administration
- **Language:** English
- **URL:** https://www.theseus.fi/handle/10024/42994
- **Description:** Examines Finland's duopolistic grocery sector and compares it with Germany's market. Evaluates whether German retailer Kaufland could viably enter Finland. Uses PEST and SWOT analysis. Identifies competition barriers and market entry feasibility. Directly addresses market concentration problem.

### 24. Factors Influencing Finnish Consumers' Grocery Shopping Decisions
- **Author:** Alvaro Martin Vadillo
- **Year:** 2026
- **Institution:** Haaga-Helia University of Applied Sciences
- **Language:** English
- **URL:** https://www.theseus.fi/handle/10024/910808
- **Description:** Very recent study examining brand trust, perceived quality, country-of-origin, and sustainability factors. Finds brand trust and country of origin are most influential, with consumers preferring local products. Notes consumers perceive price barriers to sustainable choices.

### 25. Entering the Finnish Grocery Store Market
- **Author:** Tommi Harma
- **Year:** ~2017
- **Institution:** (via Theseus.fi)
- **Programme:** Bachelor's thesis
- **Language:** English
- **URL:** https://www.theseus.fi/bitstream/handle/10024/137101/Harma_Tommi.pdf
- **Description:** Examines barriers to entering Finland's concentrated grocery market dominated by S-group and K-group. Relevant for understanding why the duopoly persists.

- **Year:** 2014

## GAPS IDENTIFIED

- **Few theses directly modeling grocery market concentration** econometrically (exceptions: Blank & Persson 2004, Myte 2012, Kronqvist 2010)
- **Iceland underrepresented** - only 2 theses found, none directly on market concentration
- **Danish market concentration** not directly studied in any thesis found (the Aalborg 2025 project comes closest)
- **No thesis found comparing all Nordic grocery markets** in a single study
- **Limited coverage of food system transition** in the context of retail concentration
- **Section 4a (Finland)** not analyzed from a thesis perspective
