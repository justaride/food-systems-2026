# Nordic Food Retail Market Structures: Comparative Research
## Denmark, Sweden, Finland (for comparison with Norway)
### Data collected: March 2026 | Sources: 2024-2025 data preferred

---

## 1. DENMARK

### 1.1 Major Grocery Chains & Parent Companies

| Group | Key Chains | Ownership | Est. Market Share (2024) |
|-------|-----------|-----------|------------------------|
| **Salling Group** | Netto, Føtex, Bilka, Salling | Private (Salling family) | ~35% |
| **Coop Danmark** | Kvickly, SuperBrugsen, Coop 365, Dagli'Brugsen | Cooperative (1.9M members) | ~28-30% |
| **Rema 1000** | Rema 1000 | Reitan Group (NO) | ~14% |
| **Lidl Denmark** | Lidl | Schwarz Group (DE) | ~5% (107 stores) |
| **Dagrofa** | MENY, SPAR | Private | ~5% |

- **Aldi exited Denmark** in January 2024: stores divided among Rema 1000, Salling Group, Coop, and Lidl
- Salling Group owns 600+ grocery stores in Denmark
- Coop Danmark operates ~900 supermarkets
- Rema 1000 operates 419 stores + 189 7-Eleven (via Reitan)

### 1.2 Market Concentration

- **Top-2 share:** ~63-65% (Salling + Coop)
- **Top-3 share:** ~77-79% (+ Rema 1000)
- **Top-5 share:** ~95%
- **Estimated HHI:** ~2,400-2,600 (lowest among Nordic countries per NHH FOOD research)
- **Number of significant players:** 5 (Salling, Coop, Rema, Lidl, Dagrofa)
- **Trend:** Denmark is the only Nordic country where grocery concentration has *decreased* over the past 20 years
- **Discount share:** ~41% of total market (projected to reach 49% by 2029)

### 1.3 Self-Sufficiency Rate

- **Overall:** ~300% (Denmark produces ~3x its domestic food needs)
- Denmark is the only Nordic-Baltic country that is a **net exporter** of agricultural products
- 62% of arable land used for food production
- **Strong in:** pork, dairy, cereals, eggs
- **Dependent on imports for:** fruits, vegetables, plant proteins for animal feed
- Leading EU pork producer

### 1.4 Competition Authority & Regulatory Bodies

- **Konkurrence- og Forbrugerstyrelsen (KFST)** — Danish Competition and Consumer Authority
- **Konkurrencerådet** — Danish Competition Council (decision-making body)
- Website: en.kfst.dk

### 1.5 Recent Competition Cases & Market Studies

- **Salling/Coop Store Acquisition (2025):** Phase II investigation opened January 7, 2025 for Salling Group's acquisition of 35 stores from Coop Danmark. Approved unconditionally March 26, 2025 (reduced to 33 stores after competition concerns in Taastrup and Slagelse led to voluntary exclusion of 2 stores)
- **OK/Coop Danmark (2024):** Competition Council intervened in OK's acquisition of Coop Danmark (June 2024)
- Intense price competition between major retailers through 2024-2025, with pressure on suppliers for price reductions

## 2. SWEDEN

### 2.1 Major Grocery Chains & Parent Companies

| Group | Key Chains | Ownership | Est. Market Share (2024) |
|-------|-----------|-----------|------------------------|
| **ICA Gruppen** | ICA Maxi, ICA Kvantum, ICA Supermarket, ICA Nära | Listed (Nasdaq Stockholm) | ~50% |
| **Axfood** | Willys, Hemköp, Tempo, City Gross (acquired Nov 2024) | Listed (Nasdaq Stockholm) | ~25% (incl. City Gross) |
| **Coop Sverige** | Coop, Stora Coop | Cooperative (consumer cooperatives) | ~14% |
| **Lidl Sweden** | Lidl | Schwarz Group (DE) | ~6-7% |

- ICA operates ~1,300 stores
- Axfood acquired City Gross (3.2% market share) for SEK 2 billion, completed November 2024
- Coop Sverige in severe crisis: SEK 2.7 billion operating loss in 2024, lost 400,000 customers in 2 years
- Swedish food retail market: ~SEK 315 billion in 2025, growing ~4.5%

### 2.2 Market Concentration

- **Top-2 share:** ~75% (ICA + Axfood)
- **Top-3 share:** ~89-91% (ICA + Axfood + Coop)
- **Top-4 share:** ~95-97% (+ Lidl)
- **Estimated HHI:** ~3,200-3,500 (second highest among Nordics, "silver" per NHH)
- **Number of significant players:** 4 (ICA, Axfood, Coop, Lidl)
- **Trend:** Concentration *increasing* — Axfood acquired City Gross (2024); Coop declining rapidly
- **Discount share:** ~25% of market (Willys showing 11% annual growth)
- Described as "oligopolistic" by Swedish Competition Authority

### 2.3 Self-Sufficiency Rate

- **Overall:** ~50%
- Lowest among Nordic countries
- Only 37% of Swedes feel secure about food supply in crisis (Lantmännen survey, August 2024)
- 90%+ of Swedes say Sweden should be more self-sufficient
- **National Food Strategy (2016/2017):** aims to increase domestic food production
- Dependent on imports for significant share of food supply

### 2.4 Competition Authority & Regulatory Bodies

- **Konkurrensverket** — Swedish Competition Authority
- Website: konkurrensverket.se
- Key legislation: Swedish Competition Act (Konkurrenslag)
- Planning and Building Act (Plannings- och bygglagen) — municipal zoning affects store establishment

### 2.5 Recent Competition Concerns & Market Studies

- **Report 2024:5 — Government-commissioned food price inquiry:**
  - Identified risk of "inadequate competition and unjustified price increases" in food industry
  - Eggs: prices increased more than justified by input costs at both packer and retail level
  - Also studied edible oil, dairy products, chicken
  - Concentration in food supply chain has increased over time
  - Few examples of new actors entering the market

- **Barriers to competition identified:**
  - Shortage of suitable sites for new grocery stores
  - Municipal planning processes make it "unnecessarily difficult" to establish new stores
  - Both municipalities and existing retailers act to restrict new entrants
  - SCA cannot intervene in local-market mergers (can only act when merger affects whole country or substantial part)

- **Axfood/City Gross acquisition (2024):** Approved by Konkurrensverket

## 3. FINLAND

### 3.1 Major Grocery Chains & Parent Companies

| Group | Key Chains | Ownership | Est. Market Share (2024) |
|-------|-----------|-----------|------------------------|
| **S Group (S-ryhmä)** | Prisma, S-Market, Sale, Alepa | Cooperative (~2.7M member-owners) | ~48.8% |
| **K Group (Kesko)** | K-Citymarket, K-Supermarket, K-Market | Listed (Nasdaq Helsinki) | ~33.7% |
| **Lidl Finland** | Lidl | Schwarz Group (DE) | ~9.4% |
| **Tokmanni** | Tokmanni | Listed | ~3.3% |
| **Minimani** | Minimani | Private | ~0.5% |
| **M-ketju** | M-ketju | Private | ~0.2% |

- S Group: customer-owned cooperative, 2.7 million members (in a country of 5.6M)
- K Group (Kesko): publicly listed, market share slightly declined in 2024
- Lidl Finland: 200+ stores, entered market in 2002
- Total market exceeds EUR 23.5 billion annually

### 3.2 Market Concentration

- **Top-2 share:** ~82.5% (S Group + K Group) — **highest duopoly concentration in Europe**
- **Top-3 share:** ~91.9% (+ Lidl)
- **Estimated HHI:** ~3,500-3,800 (highest among Nordic countries, "gold" per NHH)
- **Number of significant players:** 3 (S Group, Kesko, Lidl)
- **Trend:** Persistent high concentration; S Group has slowly increased share
- Finland has one of the most concentrated grocery retail markets in Europe
- Effective duopoly with Lidl as a distant third player

### 3.3 Self-Sufficiency Rate

- **Overall:** ~80%
- **Eggs:** ~117%
- **Bread cereals:** ~112% (but rye only 55%)
- **Poultry:** ~85%
- **Beef:** ~80%
- 40,000+ farms; 74% focused on crops, 20% on livestock (2024)
- Finland emphasizes food security/stockpiling as part of national preparedness
- Finnish food exports saw tailwinds in 2025

### 3.4 Competition Authority & Regulatory Bodies

- **Kilpailu- ja kuluttajavirasto (KKV)** — Finnish Competition and Consumer Authority (FCCA)
- Website: kkv.fi
- Key legislation: Finnish Competition Act (Kilpailulaki)
- **Special provision:** Section 4a of Competition Act (effective January 1, 2014)

### 3.5 Section 4a — Unique Grocery Trade Provision

- **30% market share threshold:** Any company with >=30% national market share in grocery retail is *automatically deemed to hold a dominant position*
- Applies to both retail and procurement markets
- Currently applies to **both S Group and K Group**
- Prohibits abuse of dominant position (same standard as EU Article 102)
- Companies must self-assess compliance
- KKV cannot issue general binding instructions; evaluates case-by-case
- **Purpose:** Protect competitive process, not individual businesses

### 3.6 Recent Market Studies

- **Food Market Functioning Study (2024):** FCCA published interim report on price margins and price transmission in the food chain, covering consumer prices, retail purchase prices, industrial sales prices, and raw material costs at product level (monthly data, Jan 2015 - April 2023)
- **Data Sharing Investigation:** FCCA investigated sharing of Kesko Food's and SOK's sales data on daily consumer goods trade
- **Loyalty schemes, supplier data access, and private label practices** under ongoing scrutiny

## 5. KEY ANALYTICAL OBSERVATIONS

### 5.1 Market Structure Patterns

1. **All Nordic grocery markets are highly concentrated by international standards** — HHIs ranging from ~2,500 (Denmark) to ~3,600 (Finland), all well above the 2,500 threshold for "highly concentrated"
2. **Each country has a single dominant player at 35-50%** — structural similarity across the region
3. **Cooperatives play a major role everywhere** — Coop (DK, SE, NO), S Group (FI) — but are declining in Sweden
4. **Lidl is the only significant international competitor** across all four markets; Aldi has exited Denmark (2024)

### 5.2 Denmark as Competitive Outlier

- Most competitive Nordic grocery market (lowest HHI)
- Only Nordic country where concentration has *decreased* over 20 years
- Highest discount penetration (~41%)
- Most major players (5 vs. 3-4)
- Major net food exporter (unique in the Nordics)

### 5.3 Finland as Concentration Extreme

- Highest concentration in Europe (effective duopoly)
- Prompted unique regulatory response: Section 4a automatic dominance provision at 30% threshold
- Both major players (S Group and K Group) are cooperative/association structures
- Despite high concentration, Lidl has gained ~10% in two decades

### 5.4 Sweden — Structural Shift Underway

- Coop Sverige in crisis (SEK 2.7B loss, declining from 22% to 14% in a decade)
- Axfood consolidating (acquired City Gross 2024)
- Market effectively transitioning from 3-player to 2-player dominance (ICA + Axfood = 75%)
- Konkurrensverket identified systemic barriers to new entry (zoning, site access)
- Lowest food self-sufficiency in the Nordics (~50%)

### 5.5 Regulatory Divergence

- **Finland** has the strictest grocery-specific rules (automatic dominance at 30%)
- **Sweden** relies on general competition law but found it insufficient for local-market interventions
- **Denmark** has no grocery-specific provisions but active Phase II merger control
- All three countries have conducted or are conducting food price/margin studies post-inflation crisis
