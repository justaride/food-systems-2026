**Executive summary **

(1/3 page)

The Nordic food system faces significant sustainability challenges related to resource efficiency, circularity, and environmental impact. Despite ambitious goals, decision-makers and businesses lack a comprehensive overview of material flows, value chains, and improvement opportunities at a Nordic level. This project aims to address this knowledge gap by conducting a Nordic-wide material flow analysis (MFA) and value chain mapping, with a focus on the most resource-intensive and impactful sectors.

The project will prioritize animal-based and plant-based production, key inputs like feed and packaging, and food waste/side stream valorization. It will quantify material flows, assess circularity potential, and identify innovation opportunities for better resource utilization and value creation. Through extensive industry engagement, the project will co-create a portfolio of 10+ circular solutions and business models tailored to the Nordic context.

Over 1 year, a consortium of leading Nordic research and innovation actors will execute the project, supported by a Nordic Food System Transition Group of 20+ industry representatives, authorities and NGOs. The project will deliver: 1\) A Nordic material flow model and circularity assessment; 2\) Industry innovation workshops and solution portfolios; 3\) Policy briefs and a Nordic roadmap for circular food.

With a budget of 4 MNOK, the project will mobilize significant own resources and pursue synergies with related initiatives to maximize impact. The primary target groups are Nordic food industry actors, entrepreneurs, and policymakers, but results will also benefit researchers, educators, and civil society.

The project's Nordic added value lies in uncovering cross-border synergies and economies of scale for the transition to a circular food system. By providing a common fact base and innovation platform, the project will enable Nordic stakeholders to join forces and develop solutions with regional and global competitiveness. This supports the realization of the Nordic Vision 2030 and the UN SDGs.

**A. Impact **

Project objectives 

(1 1/2 pages) 

The main objective of this project is to enable the Nordic countries to collaboratively address the critical sustainability challenges in the region's food systems by providing new insights into key material flows and value chains, and stimulating innovation and value creation across Nordic borders and sectors.

The food sector is one of the most important industries in the Nordics, but also one of the largest in terms of resource use, emissions, and waste. It faces significant sustainability challenges, but also great opportunities for improvement through circular and bio-based solutions. However, realizing these opportunities requires a better understanding of the current system, stronger collaboration across Nordic value chains, and joint innovation efforts targeting the most impactful areas.

This project aims to provide a Nordic-level mapping and analysis of material flows and value chains, with a focus on the most resource-intensive and environmentally burdensome sectors, including:

* Animal-based food production (meat, dairy, eggs, aquaculture)

* Plant-based food production (grains, vegetables, legumes)

* Key inputs to food production (feed, fertilizers, packaging)

* Management of food waste and side streams

By taking a Nordic approach, the project can uncover improvement opportunities that are difficult to see at a national level, and enable solutions that require collaboration and scale across Nordic borders.

To achieve this, the project has four sub-objectives:

1. Map and quantify the main material flows, waste, and side streams in prioritized Nordic food value chains, to identify inefficiencies, risks, and potential for better utilization across the Nordic region.

2. Analyze the key barriers and opportunities for increased circularity, resource efficiency, and sustainability in the mapped Nordic value chains, considering technical, economic, regulatory, and organizational aspects.

3. Assess the potential for selected circular solutions, industrial symbiosis, and bio-based value creation, and co-create 10+ specific innovation concepts through cross-Nordic industry collaboration and knowledge sharing.

4. Synthesize key findings and develop actionable recommendations for Nordic industry, policy makers, and R\&D to enable the realization of the identified opportunities and solutions, and develop a common Nordic agenda for a circular food system transition.

In a 1-year period, the project will focus on establishing a solid fact base and inspiring action, by delivering: 1\) A Nordic material flow analysis and circularity assessment report; 2\) Innovation opportunity maps and 10+ co-created solution concepts; 3\) Policy briefs and strategic recommendations for Nordic stakeholders.

The project lays the foundation for significant positive impacts on the environment, resource use, value creation, and competitiveness of the Nordic food sector. It contributes to the realization of the Nordic Vision 2030 and the UN SDGs by mobilizing Nordic collaboration and innovation for a sustainable food system transition.

Nordic added value 

(1/2 page) 

This project pioneers a Nordic approach to identify and realize the untapped potentials for sustainable value creation in the region's food system. By taking a system perspective across national borders and value chains, the project will provide new insights and inspire joint Nordic action to address shared challenges and opportunities.

Currently, the Nordic food sector is characterized by limited cross-border collaboration and synergies, with national interests, regulations, and peculiarities often prevailing. This fragmentation hinders the development and scaling of innovative solutions for circularity and sustainability. The project challenges these structures by mapping material flows, side streams, and improvement opportunities at a Nordic level.

Key Nordic added values of the project include:

* A common knowledge base for benchmarking and prioritizing actions across the Nordic food system, utilizing the region's complementary strengths and resources.

* Identification of specific opportunities for cross-border industrial symbiosis and circular value chains, e.g., using side streams from one country as input for another.

* Co-creation and scaling of circular solutions through Nordic innovation clusters and platforms, leveraging the region's strong food industry and bioeconomy competencies.

* Increased competitiveness and value creation for Nordic food companies in the growing market for sustainable products and services, by developing solutions with a "Nordic brand".

* Strengthened Nordic voice and thought leadership in the European and global agenda for sustainable food system transformation, by demonstrating the benefits of regional collaboration.

In addition, the project contributes to strengthening the resilience, self-sufficiency, and food security of the Nordic region in light of increasing global uncertainties and supply chain disruptions. By identifying opportunities for substituting imports and enhancing intra-Nordic trade, the project helps build a more robust and adaptive Nordic food system.

Ultimately, the project advances the realization of the Nordic Vision 2030 of becoming the most sustainable and integrated region in the world, by catalyzing the transition to a circular and bio-based food economy. The Nordic learnings and solutions developed can serve as inspiration for other regions pursuing similar goals.

**B. Quality and Relevance **

Background and relevance 

(1 page) 

The Nordic food system faces urgent sustainability challenges that require a coordinated and knowledge-based approach to solve. Despite ambitious policies and goals, there is a lack of a comprehensive overview and understanding of the material flows, value chains, and improvement opportunities at a Nordic level. This project addresses this critical knowledge gap to catalyze the necessary transition to a more circular and sustainable Nordic food system.

Key challenges and drivers include:

1. High environmental footprint: The Nordic food sector is a major contributor to climate emissions, biodiversity loss, and resource depletion, with significant untapped potential for circularity and resource efficiency.

2. Fragmented value chains: Current linear structures, silo thinking, and suboptimal cooperation lead to overuse of resources, high levels of waste and limited value creation across Nordic borders.

3. Import dependency and vulnerabilities: Nordic food production relies heavily on imported inputs such as feed, fertilizers, and fossil fuels, creating vulnerabilities to global supply disruptions and price volatility.

4. Urgent policy goals and commitments: The food sector is critical for achieving the ambitious goals of the Nordic Vision 2030, European Green Deal, and UN SDGs, but requires a rapid and coordinated transformation.

At the same time, the Nordic region has unique strengths and opportunities to address these challenges:

1. Strong food and bioeconomy sectors: The Nordics have globally leading companies and competencies in sustainable food production, processing, and packaging that can be leveraged for circular solutions.

2. Common values and collaboration culture: The shared Nordic heritage, values, and collaboration models provide a strong foundation for co-creating and implementing innovative solutions across borders.

3. Growing market for sustainable food: Nordic consumers and export markets increasingly demand sustainable and healthy food products, creating new business opportunities for circular and plant-based solutions.

4. Supportive policy frameworks: Nordic policies and strategies prioritize the green transition, bioeconomy, and circular solutions, providing a conducive environment for food system transformation.

The project is highly relevant and timely for the Nordic region, as it:

* Provides a holistic and cross-sectoral mapping of material flows and value chains in the Nordic food system, enabling better prioritization and coordination of efforts.

* Identifies specific opportunities for increased circularity, resource efficiency, and value creation in the prioritized sectors and value chains, through Nordic collaboration and innovation.

* Engages key industry, policy, and research stakeholders from all Nordic countries to co-create and implement solutions, leveraging the region's complementary strengths and resources.

* Contributes to the realization of the Nordic Vision 2030 and other key policies by providing the necessary knowledge base and collaboration platform for a sustainable food system transition.

By taking a Nordic approach, the project can overcome current barriers and silos, and unlock the full potential for sustainable value creation in the region's food sector. The project results will not only benefit the Nordic region, but also serve as inspiration for other regions facing similar challenges and opportunities in the transition to a circular and sustainable food system.

Business plan and opportunities 

(1 page) 

The project's business plan focuses on translating the insights and recommendations from the analysis into tangible business opportunities and initiatives that can drive the circular transformation of the Nordic food sector. Key elements include:

1. Identification of circular business opportunities: The project will map specific underutilized resources, side streams, and improvement potentials in the prioritized Nordic food sectors and value chains (e.g., dairy, meat, aquaculture, plant-based). These will be evaluated and prioritized based on their potential for circular value creation, scalability, and Nordic synergies.

2. Co-creation of circular business models and solutions: In close collaboration with Nordic industry partners and entrepreneurs, the project will co-create a portfolio of 10-15 innovative business concepts that can turn the identified opportunities into viable circular solutions. These may include new products, services, technologies, and collaboration models that can be tested and refined with key stakeholders.

3. Stimulation of circular entrepreneurship and innovation: The project will actively engage and support Nordic entrepreneurs, start-ups, and SMEs in developing and scaling circular food solutions, by providing them with market insights, partnership opportunities, and visibility. A Nordic circular food innovation challenge will be organized to identify and promote the most promising ideas and teams.

4. Development of Nordic circular food clusters and brand: The project will facilitate the formation and strengthening of cross-Nordic clusters and ecosystems that can pool resources, knowledge, and infrastructures to develop world-class circular food solutions. Joint branding and storytelling activities will be conducted to position the Nordic circular food sector as a global frontrunner and attract international partners and investors.

Within the 1-year project period, the following key outputs and outcomes will be delivered:

* A Nordic portfolio of 10-15 co-created circular food business concepts and models

* A Nordic circular food innovation challenge with 25+ participating start-ups and SMEs

* Establishment or strengthening of 3-5 Nordic circular food clusters and partnerships

* International communication and branding of Nordic circular food solutions at 2-3 major events and platforms

To realize the full business potential of the project, continuation activities are envisioned such as:

* Piloting and commercialization of the most promising circular food solutions and technologies

* Establishment of a Nordic circular food accelerator program and venture fund

* Development of Nordic circular food standards, labels, and procurement criteria

* Capacity building and skills development programs for circular food entrepreneurship and innovation

* Policy dialogues and public-private partnerships to create enabling framework conditions

These activities will be detailed and pursued in the upscaling phase of the project, in close dialogue with key Nordic and international stakeholders. The aim is to mobilize a strong coalition and momentum for the circular transformation of the Nordic food sector, with significant economic, environmental, and social benefits.

**Risk assessment **

(3/4 page) 

The Nordic material flow analysis for the food system project acknowledges several potential risks that may impact its success, and have developed mitigation measures. These include:

| Main risks | Mitigation |
| :---- | :---- |
| **1\. Data availability and quality:** The project heavily relies on access to comprehensive and reliable data on material flows, waste streams, and resource use in the Nordic food system. Inconsistencies, gaps, or limitations in data quality may hinder the accuracy and usefulness of the analyses. | The project will collaborate closely with national statistical agencies, research institutions, and industry partners to identify and access the best available data sources. The methodology will include robust data validation and quality assurance procedures. |
| **2\. Stakeholder engagement and buy-in:** The project's success depends on the active participation and support of a wide range of stakeholders across the Nordic food system. Lack of engagement, conflicting interests, or resistance to change may limit the project's impact and implementation of recommendations. | The project will establish a dedicated Transition Group on Circularity in Nordic Food Systems and employ participatory methods to ensure continuous stakeholder involvement and ownership. Clear communication of benefits, best practices, and success stories will help build momentum and support for the project's objectives. |
| **3\. Complexity and scope:** The Nordic food system is a complex, multi-faceted, and interconnected network of actors, flows, and processes. The project's ambitious scope and timeline may prove challenging in terms of managing complexity, ensuring depth of analysis, and delivering actionable results. | The project will adopt a phased approach, using the general mapping to identify and focus on selected high-impact value chains and material streams, and based on this develop the ten cross-nordic innovation concepts. Collaboration with experienced research partners and advisors will help navigate complexity and maintain scientific rigor. |
| **4\. Resource constraints and competing priorities:** The project's budget and human resources are limited, and partners may face competing demands and priorities within their organizations. This could lead to delays, reduced quality, or lack of follow-through on implementation. | The project will establish clear roles, responsibilities, clear plans and expectations for all partners, and closely monitor progress and resource use. Synergies with ongoing initiatives and funding sources will be actively pursued to maximize impact and efficiency. |

* Initiate implementation in selected value chains through innovation projects and pilot initiatives

* Ensure competence transfer and continuation of project results in Nordic institutions and networks

* Deliverables: Synthesis and recommendation report, communication products, roadmap for implementation, initiated innovation projects

* Cross-case analysis and synthesis of key findings and recommendations

* Scenario development and roadmapping workshops with Transition Group

* Stakeholder dialogue and dissemination events to share results and build momentum for implementation

* Planning for post-project continuation and scale-up of activities

**Plan of Action (with a list of key deliverables and milestones) **

The Nordic material flow analysis for the food system project will be carried out over a period of one year, with the possibility of continuation after a review of the first year's results. The project will be structured into three main phases, each with specific deliverables and milestones.

Phase 1 \- Mapping and method development (Months 1-4): Key deliverables:

* Partnership agreements

* Knowledge status report

* Methodology report

* Pilot analysis report

Milestones:

* Project kick-off meeting and establishment of Transition Group

* Literature review and data inventory completed

* Stakeholder workshops and interviews conducted

* Analytical framework and tools developed and tested

* Case studies and value chains selected for in-depth analysis

Phase 2 \- Conducting material flow analyses (Months 5-8): Key deliverables:

* Detailed material flow analyses (6-15 pcs)

* Database of Nordic material streams

* Map of side streams

Milestones:

* Data collection and analysis for selected case studies and value chains completed

* Stakeholder workshops and site visits conducted

* Material flow models and visualizations developed

* Improvement opportunities and circular solutions identified

* Material flow database and side stream map compiled

Phase 3 \- Synthesis and implementation (Months 9-12): Key deliverables:

* Synthesis and recommendation report

* Communication products (e.g., policy briefs, infographics, website)

* Roadmap for implementation

* Initiated innovation projects

Milestones:

* Cross-case analysis and synthesis of key findings and recommendations completed

* Scenario development and roadmapping workshops with Transition Group conducted

* Stakeholder dialogue and dissemination events held

* Communication products developed and disseminated

* Innovation projects and pilot initiatives initiated in selected value chains

* Capacity building and knowledge transfer activities conducted

* Post-project continuation and scale-up plan developed

Throughout the project, the following ongoing activities and milestones will ensure effective coordination, stakeholder engagement, and quality assurance:

* Regular Transition Group meetings (every 2-3 months)

* Progress reports and steering committee meetings (every 6 months)

* Continuous communication and outreach activities (website, social media, newsletters)

* Participation in relevant Nordic and international events and networks

* Collaboration with related projects and initiatives in the Nordic region and beyond

By following this plan of action and achieving the outlined deliverables and milestones, the project will generate valuable insights, tools, and momentum for the transition to a more sustainable and circular Nordic food system within the one-year timeframe. The results and experiences from the first year will lay the foundation for potential continuation and scale-up of the project in subsequent years.
